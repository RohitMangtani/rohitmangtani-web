## Structure

### Section 1: The Misfire Rate
Opens with learning a new skill. Early on, you misfire constantly (20%). The gap between getting good and plateauing is regime detection — recognizing when conditions shifted and your current approach stopped working. Maps to ML: models learn by detecting regime shifts and going to safe mode, reducing misfires from 20% to 3%. Not smarter. Better at knowing when to stop and protect.

### Section 2: The Skill Behind the Skill
Zooms out. Every skill sharpens through hardening the detection layer, not raw repetition. Good decision-makers recognize faster when the situation changed and their framework stopped working. They go safe, pull back to first principles, observe, then re-engage. People who never learn this keep pushing old approaches into new regimes. Closes with conviction.
